The Communication Struggle for Deaf Children

Deaf children (ages 5–17) face real-time communication challenges in schools, homes, and public spaces. This leads to social isolation, emotional struggles, and educational setbacks. Existing tools are often expensive, bulky, and not child-friendly. There’s a critical need for an affordable, portable, and eco-friendly solution to bridge this gap

Case Study: Bhiwandi Landfill Fire

The 2016 Bhiwandi landfill fire in Maharashtra highlighted the consequences of inadequate waste management. Triggered by methane buildup, the fire produced toxic smoke for weeks, affecting nearby communities and causing respiratory issues. Unlike traditional methods, our approach focuses on early detection and proactive management to prevent such crises.

Our Solution

Our solution is a pair of smart glasses designed to bridge the communication gap for deaf children. The glasses use a transparent OLED display to convert spoken words into real-time text, ensuring easy communication in any setting. Built with eco-friendly, recycled materials, the lightweight design is durable and comfortable for everyday use. Cost-efficient components like the ESP32 processor and MEMS microphone keep the price under ₹1500, making it accessible to more families. A built-in blink detection system enables hands-free interaction, adding convenience. Compact and portable, the glasses are tailored to meet the needs of children aged 5 to 17.
